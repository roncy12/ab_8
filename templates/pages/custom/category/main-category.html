Frequently Asked Questions

CBD oil is made by extracting CBD isolates from industrial hemp and then infused into a carrier oil. There are many ways to extract CBD isolates from raw hemp material – some methods allow the retention of other beneficial cannabinoids and terpenes. The carrier oil is usually a type of coconut-based oil or palm kernel oil.

There are several different ways you can use CBD oil, depending on your purpose for using it. You can use CBD for temporary relief from pain and soreness by applying a generous amount of topical cream into the skin or soaking in water with a bath bomb. Or CBD could be helpful for general wellness with a few tincture drops or a couple of capsules in the morning or evening. And for sleep, a dropper full from a CBD sleep aid or a softgel before bed may help.

However, consistency is essential for achieving the best results; you should take CBD daily for at least 30 days to know how well it works for you

CBD oil works by interacting with a bodily system known as the endocannabinoid system (or ECS). The primary function of our ECS is to maintain homeostasis, which is our bodies' natural balance to perform and operate efficiently against external and internal stressors.
